Bifuractions of the logistic map

In 1976, Robert May wrote a paper that always strucks me as one of the best piece of theoretical ecology[1]. I replicated part of his results in this Shiny app that shows the dynamic map of a population subject to logistic growth and the bifurcations of such dynamics. A more detailed explanation of what is happening and why can be found on my blog: popecology.

[1] May, R. M. 1976. Simple mathematical models with very complicated dynamics. Nature, 261: 459-467.
