Bifuractions of the logistic map

In 1976, Robert May wrote a paper that always strucks me as one of the best piece of theoretical ecology ever written [1]. I replicated part of his results in this Shiny app that shows the dynamic map of a population subject to logistic growth and the bifurcations of such dynamics. A more detailed explanation of what is happening and why can be found on my blog [2].

[1] May, R. M. 1976. Simple mathematical models with very complicated dynamics. Nature, 261: 459-467.

[2] https://popecology.home.blog/2020/01/18/1-2-4-chaos
